The problem

The help exists. Getting it doesn't.

A new mother in rural India is entitled to cash, free care, and her baby's first health checks. But the schemes are scattered, the rules are confusing, the deadlines are silent, and the people who need them most are the least likely to be online. So the money sits unspent and the family goes without.

Scattered

Dozens of schemes, no single view

Central and state benefits live across different departments, portals, and PDFs. No one tells a mother which ones are hers.

Silent

Deadlines pass without warning

Register the birth by day 21. Claim cash before day 270. Miss the date and the entitlement is simply gone.

Reactive

Today's tools wait to be asked

Apps and chatbots only help if you already know what to search for. The people most in need never do.

The insight

The delivery network already exists.

For every birth, a trusted health worker — the ASHA — already visits the mother on a fixed schedule: days 3, 7, 14, 21, 28 and 42. Those visit days line up almost exactly with the benefit deadlines. Sahej doesn't build a new network or a new habit. It puts a smart checklist in the hand of someone who's already at the door.

How it works

Three steps, on the phone she already carries.

No training course, no paperwork, no internet required for the basics. Built for a cheap Android phone in a village with one bar of signal.

1

Log the birth

The ASHA enters a few details about the mother and baby in seconds — state, delivery, child number, and the situation.

2

Get her plan

Sahej instantly builds this mother's personalized list: every benefit she qualifies for, the cash amount, the documents, and the deadline — laid out on her visit schedule.

3

Track to the finish

Tick off each step across the six visits. Sahej flags what's overdue, what's due soon, and what's blocked — until the money actually lands.

Coverage

Built for every real case — not just the easy one.

Welfare rules are full of exceptions, and the exceptions are exactly where families fall through. Sahej is designed around them: all 28 states and 8 union territories, central and state schemes, and the hard human situations.

36 states & UTs PMMVY · JSY · JSSK Immunization · RBSK Birth & death registration Twins & multiples Premature / low birth weight Stillbirth & infant loss Maternal death → family benefit Single mothers BPL & SC/ST rules Migrant mothers State opt-outs (e.g. West Bengal)

Every rule carries its source and a confidence flag. Items that need a human to confirm against the current Government Order are shown as “verify”, never hidden. Sahej is decision-support for a trained worker — not legal advice.
